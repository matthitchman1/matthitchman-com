About

I'm Matt. I work in tech, not engineering — and I'm learning AI in public.

I'm based in New Zealand. I work on the sales side of a big tech company, which means I spend my days talking about AI with customers. A couple of years ago I realised that to talk about it credibly, I needed to actually build with it. So I started. This blog is where I write about what I find.

What this site is.

A place for honest writing about AI, aimed at curious professionals who want to understand and build with it but don't have a computer science degree. I'm one of those people. I write for them.

Every post is tied to something I've actually shipped. No theory-only content. If I'm writing about RAG, it's because I built something with RAG. If I'm writing about MCP, it's because Claude on my laptop is pulling data from a Mac mini at home via an MCP I wrote. The build receipts are the whole point.

The voice.

A smart mate explaining something over a coffee — not a professor behind a podium. Confident but not cocky. Curious, not preachy. I'll tell you what I got wrong, what I don't understand yet, and what surprised me. That's the most useful thing I can offer.

How to reach me.

Email is the fastest: matt@matthitchman.com. I read every message. I reply to most.

Location

New Zealand. Mostly remote. Occasionally jet-lagged.

Day job

AI sales at a big-tech company. I help customers figure out what's real vs. what's hype.

Side work

Building personal AI tools in Python. Mostly agents, MCPs, and small automations that make my life easier.

Current stack

Python · Claude · MCP · Cloudflare · Azure · A lot of markdown.

Experience level

Beginner-to-intermediate coder. I ship things anyway. That's the whole point.

What I don't do

Thought-leadership takes. Enterprise AI PowerPoints. "Leveraging cutting-edge solutions."
